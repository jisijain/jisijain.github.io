Background

Rhea is a creative professional who spends long hours illustrating and designing. Music is her constant companion — she leans on it to set the mood for deep-focus work and to unwind after it.

Goals
  • Mood-based music for different contexts
  • Discover regional & indie music easily
  • Seamless cross-device listening experience
  • Fresh and engaging recommendations
Frustration
  • Repetitive recommendations on existing apps
  • Hard-to-find regional music
  • Overwhelming, cluttered UI in payments
  • Complex navigation in some apps

Rohan Verma

COLLEGE STUDENT
More Information
Age20
StatusSingle
EducationB.Tech, 2nd Yr
LocationPune
Background

Rohan soundtracks every part of his day — workouts, commutes, late-night study sessions. He jumps genres by mood, finds new tracks through reels and friends' playlists, and loses patience the moment an ad kills the energy mid-song.

Goals
  • Trending & viral songs in one tap
  • Affordable, student-friendly premium
  • Quick mood-based playlists for any moment
  • Share and listen with friends together
Frustration
  • Frequent ads interrupting playback
  • Premium plans feel too expensive
  • Same songs recommended on repeat
  • Too many steps to find a trending track
Problem

Indian listeners discover music by mood, moment and cultural context — but existing apps surface the same songs, ignore context, and interrupt the experience with poorly timed ads.

Decisions
  • Mood-first navigation — one tap, no search
  • AI recommendations shaped by time of day and listening history
  • Scroll-card format to surface new artists without effort
  • Student-tier pricing with social playlist sharing
Outcomes
  • 12 screens from research to hi-fi in 2 weeks
  • Visual language distinct from Spotify and Apple Music
  • End-to-end concept: sign-up → mood → feed → player → premium
